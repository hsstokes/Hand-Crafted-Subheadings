What is This?

You will make stamped letters to use as subheadings on your portfolio pages. These letters have texture and character. They introduce the focus of each page.

How to use them: Small subheadings that introduce each section of your research. Think poetic, not descriptive. A short title or quote that captures your idea.

HOMEWORK: Stamp Your Letters

Do this before class. Bring your scanned template.

Steps

1
Download Template

Go to Calligraphr.com and download a template. Choose only the letters you need.

2
Stamp Letters

Use your foam stamps from Session 2 (or make new ones). Stamp each letter into a template box.

3
Scan

Scan at 300 DPI. Save as JPEG or PNG. Bring to class.

TASK 1: Build Your Alphabet File

Cut out each letter and save in one PSD file with layers.

Video Tutorial

Photoshop Steps

1
Open and Clean

Open your scan. Use Image > Adjustments > Levels to make the background white and ink dark.

2
New File

File > New — make it 2000 x 2000 pixels with transparent background.

3
Copy Each Letter

For each letter:

  • Select with Rectangular Marquee
  • Copy (Ctrl+C) and paste (Ctrl+V) into new file
  • Use Magic Wand to select white, then delete
  • Name the layer (A, B, C...)
4
Save as PSD

File > Save As — choose Photoshop (.PSD). This keeps your layers.

TASK 2: Create a Subheading

Use your alphabet to spell a short subheading for a portfolio page.

Think poetic, not descriptive. Choose a short title or quote that captures your research focus. Something evocative that introduces your ideas on that page.

Steps

1
Open Both Files

Open your alphabet PSD and your portfolio page.

2
Copy Letters

From your alphabet file, select each letter layer, copy (Ctrl+C) and paste (Ctrl+V) into your portfolio page.

3
Arrange

Use Move Tool (V) to position. Use Ctrl+T to resize. Keep it sensitive to your page — not too big.

💡 Keep it subtle

Your subheading should sit sensitively on the page. It introduces your research focus without dominating. The handmade texture adds character.
